# Comics — Editorial Comic Strips Workflow

> Sophisticated multi-panel editorial storytelling.
> Think New Yorker, not Sunday funnies. Sharp, observational, architectural.

## Overview

These are editorial comic strips — 3-4 panels telling a pointed story about technology, work, or ideas. The style is sophisticated and angular: geometric character construction, minimal background detail, selective color. Characters are built from planes and angles, not rounded cartoon forms.

The humor (when present) is dry and observational, not slapstick. The tone can also be poignant, ironic, or absurdist. Every strip has a clear narrative arc with a turn in the final panel.

---

## Character Design: Angular Planeform Aesthetic

### Construction Principles

Characters are built from **geometric planes**, not organic curves:

1. **Head:** Slightly rectangular or angular oval. Jaw defined by a plane change, not a smooth curve.
2. **Body:** Broad flat planes for torso. Shoulders as angular brackets, not rounded.
3. **Limbs:** Simplified to straight segments with angular joints.
4. **Hands:** Suggested with 2-3 angular shapes, not detailed fingers.
5. **Eyes:** Simple dots or dashes within the face plane. Expressive through position, not detail.
6. **Hair:** Geometric blocks or sharp angles, not flowing lines.

### Character Color Coding

| Role | Primary Color | Secondary Color |
|---|---|---|
| **Protagonist** | Coral (#D67056) accent | Dark Charcoal (#222222) line work |
| **Secondary/Foil** | Teal (#70B8AD) accent | Dark Charcoal (#222222) line work |
| **Authority figure** | Dark Grey Blue (#2C313A) | Muted Blue (#5D95C6) shadow |
| **Environment/Object** | Tan/Sand (#E6CCAB) | Warm Cream (#F6F3EB) background |

### Expression Through Geometry

Since characters are geometric, emotions are conveyed through:
- **Angle of head plane** — tilted = confused, straight = confident
- **Eye position** — high in face = surprise, centered = neutral, narrow gap = skeptical
- **Body plane angle** — leaning forward = engaged, leaning back = dismissive
- **Shoulder angle** — raised = stressed, dropped = relaxed, asymmetric = uncertain
- **Hand gesture** — open plane = explaining, closed = frustrated

## Panel Structure

### 3-Panel Strip (Standard)

```
┌──────────┬──────────┬──────────┐
│          │          │          │
│ SETUP    │ DEVELOP  │  TURN    │
│          │          │          │
│          │          │          │
└──────────┴──────────┴──────────┘
```

- **Panel 1 (Setup):** Establish the situation, introduce characters
- **Panel 2 (Development):** Escalate, complicate, add information
- **Panel 3 (Turn):** Punchline, reversal, realization, or poignant beat

Aspect ratio for full strip: **3:1** (three squares side by side)

### 4-Panel Strip (Extended)

```
┌──────────┬──────────┬──────────┬──────────┐
│          │          │          │          │
│ SETUP    │ BUILD    │ ESCALATE │  TURN    │
│          │          │          │          │
└──────────┴──────────┴──────────┴──────────┘
```

- **Panel 1 (Setup):** Establish situation
- **Panel 2 (Build):** First complication
- **Panel 3 (Escalate):** Stakes increase, tension peaks
- **Panel 4 (Turn):** Resolution, punchline, or subversion

Aspect ratio for full strip: **4:1** or **2:1** (2x2 grid)

### 2x2 Grid (Alternative 4-Panel)

```
┌──────────┬──────────┐
│ SETUP    │ BUILD    │
│          │          │
├──────────┼──────────┤
│ ESCALATE │  TURN    │
│          │          │
└──────────┴──────────┘
```

Aspect ratio: **1:1** — good for social media.

## Narrative Patterns

### Pattern 1: The Reversal
Setup presents expectation → Turn subverts it.
*"We hired a 10x developer" → Panel 3: They 10x'd the technical debt*

### Pattern 2: The Escalation
Each panel amplifies the same problem until absurd.
*Meeting to discuss meetings → Meeting about the meeting about meetings*

### Pattern 3: The Mirror
Panel 1 and Panel 3 are visually identical but contextually different.
*"I love coding" (morning, fresh) → same pose → "I love coding" (midnight, broken)*

### Pattern 4: The Observer
A silent character watches others, final panel is their reaction.
*Two developers argue about tabs vs spaces → Observer uses a language with no indentation*

### Pattern 5: The Literal
A metaphor is taken literally with deadpan delivery.
*"Let's break down the monolith" → characters with actual sledgehammers*

### Pattern 6: The Poignant
No joke — an emotional or philosophical observation across panels.
*An old codebase's last deploy, growing dark, a single comment left behind*

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: STORY

**Goal:** Define the narrative in script form.

Write the strip as a script:

```
PANEL 1:
  [Scene description]
  CHARACTER A: "Dialogue"
  CHARACTER B: "Dialogue"

PANEL 2:
  [Scene description]
  CHARACTER A: "Dialogue"

PANEL 3:
  [Scene description]
  [Beat / reaction / punchline]
  CHARACTER B: "Dialogue"
```

Rules:
- Maximum 2 speech bubbles per panel
- Maximum 15 words per speech bubble
- The turn panel can have zero dialogue (silent beat)
- Every strip needs a clear "before" and "after" the turn

---

### Step 2: STAGING

**Goal:** Plan the visual composition of each panel.

For each panel, define:

```
Panel [N]:
  Camera: [close-up / medium / wide / overhead]
  Characters: [who is visible, positions]
  Protagonist accent: Coral (#D67056) on [what element]
  Secondary accent: Teal (#70B8AD) on [what element]
  Background: [minimal / environmental detail / none]
  Speech bubbles: [position, tail direction]
  Key emotion: [what the reader should feel]
```

#### Staging Principles

1. **Vary the camera** — don't use the same shot for every panel
2. **Panel 3 should have the strongest composition** — it carries the weight
3. **Use negative space in the turn panel** — let the moment breathe
4. **Coral accents intensify toward the turn** — visual build-up
5. **Backgrounds simplify as tension increases** — by the final panel, environment fades

---

### Step 3: DIALOGUE DESIGN

**Goal:** Finalize speech bubble content and visual text treatment.

#### Speech Bubble Style

- **Shape:** Rounded rectangle with slight hand-drawn wobble, not perfect ovals
- **Tail:** Angular pointer (not curved), aimed at speaker's head
- **Fill:** Warm Cream (#F6F3EB) or white
- **Border:** Dark Charcoal (#222222), thin hand-drawn line
- **Font:** Clean monospace technical font (JetBrains Mono feel)
- **Text color:** Dark Charcoal (#222222)
- **Emphasis:** Bold text in Coral (#D67056) for stressed words

#### Special Bubble Types

| Type | Style | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Thought** | Dashed border, cloud shape | Internal monologue |
| **Whisper** | Smaller text, dashed border, tiny tail | Quiet aside |
| **Shout** | Jagged border, larger bold text | Exclamation |
| **Code** | Monospace block inside bubble, Dark Grey Blue (#2C313A) background | Showing code |
| **Narration** | No tail, rectangular box at top of panel | Scene-setting text |

---

### Step 4: PROMPT CONSTRUCTION

**Goal:** Build the generation prompt for each panel or the full strip.

#### Full Strip Prompt Template (Preferred)

```
Editorial comic strip, [3/4] panels in a horizontal row on warm cream (#F6F3EB) background.

Style: Sophisticated editorial illustration. Angular planeform characters built from geometric shapes — rectangular heads, angular shoulders, simplified limbs. NOT cartoonish or rounded. Think New Yorker editorial style. Clean monospace technical font like JetBrains Mono for all text.

Panel 1 — [CAMERA]:
[SCENE DESCRIPTION]
Speech bubble: "[DIALOGUE]"
Character accent: coral (#D67056) on [protagonist element]
Teal (#70B8AD) on [secondary element]

Panel 2 — [CAMERA]:
[SCENE DESCRIPTION]
Speech bubble: "[DIALOGUE]"
[COLOR NOTES]

Panel 3 — [CAMERA]:
[SCENE DESCRIPTION]
[Speech bubble or silent beat]
[COLOR NOTES]
[If 4 panels, add Panel 4]

Consistent character design across all panels. Dark charcoal (#222222) line work throughout. Panels separated by thin charcoal borders. Warm cream (#F6F3EB) panel backgrounds. Minimal background detail — the characters and dialogue are the focus.

Both coral (#D67056) and teal (#70B8AD) must appear in the strip.
```

### Step 7: VALIDATE

**Goal:** Verify the comic strip meets all quality and brand standards.

#### Validation Checklist

**Narrative:**
- [ ] Clear narrative arc: setup → development → turn
- [ ] The turn (final panel) delivers a satisfying payoff
- [ ] Dialogue is concise (max 15 words per bubble)
- [ ] No more than 2 speech bubbles per panel
- [ ] Story is comprehensible without external context

**Character Design:**
- [ ] Characters use angular planeform aesthetic (not rounded cartoon)
- [ ] Consistent character design across all panels
- [ ] Protagonist has Coral (#D67056) accent elements
- [ ] Secondary character has Teal (#70B8AD) accent elements
- [ ] Expressions are readable through geometric means

**Visual Style:**
- [ ] Sophisticated editorial style (New Yorker quality, not cartoonish)
- [ ] Dark Charcoal (#222222) primary line work
- [ ] Warm Cream (#F6F3EB) panel backgrounds
- [ ] Clean panel borders
- [ ] Minimal, intentional background detail
- [ ] Both Coral and Teal present in the strip

**Typography:**
- [ ] Speech bubble text is legible
- [ ] Monospace font styling for all text
- [ ] Emphasis words in Coral where appropriate
- [ ] Bubble shapes have hand-drawn character (not perfectly geometric)

**Composition:**
- [ ] Camera angles vary between panels
- [ ] Final panel has strongest composition
- [ ] Negative space used effectively
- [ ] Reading flow is clear (left to right, top to bottom)
- [ ] Panels are evenly sized (or intentionally varied)

**Technical:**
- [ ] Web-optimized version created
- [ ] WebP conversion completed
- [ ] All files in `~/prj/art-skill/outputs/`
- [ ] Text is readable at web size

#### Failure Protocol

| Issue | Fix |
|---|---|
| Characters inconsistent across panels | Generate per-panel with explicit character description, then stitch |
| Dialogue illegible | Reduce text, increase bubble size, or add text post-generation |
| Style too cartoonish | Emphasize "angular", "geometric", "editorial", "New Yorker" in prompt |
| Turn doesn't land | Rewrite the script — the story problem precedes the art problem |
| Colors missing | Explicitly name hex values for each panel element in the prompt |

## Examples

### Example 1: The Standup Meeting (3 panels, The Escalation)

**Script:**
```
PANEL 1 — Medium shot:
  Angular figure at desk, looking at screen.
  DEVELOPER: "Quick standup, 15 minutes max."

PANEL 2 — Wide shot:
  Same figure, now surrounded by 8 angular figures in a circle.
  MANAGER: "Let's also discuss the Q3 roadmap."

PANEL 3 — Close-up:
  Clock showing 2 hours later. Developer's angular head tilted down.
  [Silent beat — no dialogue]
  Small text at bottom: "it was not quick."
```

**Prompt:**
```
Editorial comic strip, 3 panels horizontal on warm cream (#F6F3EB) background.

Style: Sophisticated editorial. Angular planeform characters — geometric heads, angular shoulders, simplified forms. New Yorker editorial quality. Clean monospace technical font like JetBrains Mono.

Panel 1 — Medium shot: Single angular figure at desk with screen. Speech bubble: "Quick standup, 15 minutes max." Coral (#D67056) accent on the figure's shirt.

Panel 2 — Wide shot: Same figure now surrounded by 8 angular figures in a standing circle. Speech bubble from a taller authority figure: "Let's also discuss the Q3 roadmap." Teal (#70B8AD) accent on the surrounding figures.

Panel 3 — Close-up on a clock showing 2:00, and the developer's angular head tilted downward in defeat. No speech bubble. Small monospace text at bottom: "it was not quick." Mustard yellow (#EAB64D) accent on the clock hands.

Dark charcoal (#222222) line work. Thin charcoal panel borders. Minimal backgrounds. Both coral and teal present.
```

### Example 2: The Code Review (4 panels, The Mirror)

**Script:**
```
PANEL 1 — Medium:
  Developer looking at code, confident expression (angular head straight).
  DEVELOPER: "Who wrote this garbage?"

PANEL 2 — Close-up on screen:
  Git blame results visible.
  [No dialogue]

PANEL 3 — Close-up on developer's face:
  Eyes widened (dots moved higher in face plane).
  [No dialogue]

PANEL 4 — Same as Panel 1, exact mirror:
  Same developer, same pose, deflated shoulders.
  DEVELOPER: "Ah."
```
