Lesson 0002 · Astronomy

Moon phases without shadow myths

Toward your mission: explain a sky pattern by reading the geometry that causes it.

By the end, you can look at the Moon's shape and decide whether the lit part is growing or shrinking.

The cause

The Moon is always half lit by the Sun. A phase is the slice of that lit half we can see from Earth as the Moon moves around us.

1 Earth's shadow is not the cause of ordinary phases. That shadow matters during a lunar eclipse, when the Sun, Earth, and Moon line up closely enough.

So the useful question is not "what shape is the Moon?" It is "where is the Moon compared with the Sun, as seen from Earth?"

The cycle rail
  1. New

    lit side points mostly away from Earth

  2. First quarter

    right half lit in a Northern Hemisphere view

  3. Full

    lit side faces Earth

  4. Last quarter

    left half lit in a Northern Hemisphere view

Name what changes
Same geometry, three views

The Moon is near the Sun in our sky. The lit half mostly faces away from us, so the Moon is hard to see.

The Moon is about a quarter of the way around its orbit from new. We see half of its lit half.

The Moon is opposite the Sun in our sky. The lit half faces Earth, so the disk looks full.

A compact field rule
Recall by ordering

Put these primary phases in order after new moon.

  1. Full moon
  2. First quarter
  3. Last quarter

After new moon, the visible lit part grows until full, then shrinks.

Answer: first quarter, full moon, last quarter.

Once the order is stable, switch from the cycle to the vocabulary cues inside each name.

Recall by pairing

Match each phase word to the cue it gives you.

Word
Cue

waxing = growing; waning = shrinking; gibbous = more than half lit.

Now use those cues on a sky observation before opening the explanation.

Predict before reveal

A right-lit crescent is visible after sunset in a Northern Hemisphere view. Predict the direction word.

Answer: waxing.

After sunset, the Moon is east of the Sun in the sky. The lit slice grows on the way to full moon.

Finish by rating whether the geometry itself feels usable.
